# Sadguru Ganapathi Swamigal

In Thattanchavady, Puducherry, there is a temple that holds the Samadhi of Sadguru Ganapathi Swamigal , a master who lived 121 years, who walked the path of the Siddhas, and whose presence, devotees say, continues to guide those who come to sit before him.

---

## Born into seeking

Ganapathi Swamigal was born on 21 August 1879 in Sekkadi Kuppam, in the old Vellore district. From childhood, the draw toward God was strong. He found his Sadguru in Arulananda Swamigal, received Thiruvadi Diksha, and followed the teachings until he had fully realised what they pointed to.

In time, he gave away his property, left his home, and moved among his devotees. When those from Puducherry began travelling far to receive Diksha, he told them simply: *"Rather than so many of you coming to me , I will come to you."* He arrived in Puducherry in 1996 and never left.

---

## His teaching

Swamigal taught what the Siddhas have long taught , that the Self is without birth or death, and that it can be known directly. Through Thiruvadi Diksha, the disciple is shown their own soul. The practice he gave was not difficult. It was simple, gentle, and he made one promise to those who followed it: *"I will take you to Shiva."*

Those who came to him found he could speak of their past lives in detail , what had been carried, what still remained. Like Swami Vallalar, his teaching pointed always to the same light: *Jothi. Jothi. Jothi.*

---

## The Samadhi

On 29 May 2001, Swamigal entered his Samadhi. His devotees are clear on what this means , not death, but a conscious, deliberate withdrawal into total stillness. Breath ceases. Circulation ceases. The master enters knowingly. Only then is the body interred , and it remains, preserved, for however many years follow.

When the airport expansion required the land of his original ashram, the Samadhi was carefully relocated. On 10 January 2011, with government support and police escort, it was brought to Thattanchavady. As the Samadhi was lifted, one devotee reached in to clear obstructing sand and touched the foot of Swamigal , ten years after his passing, unchanged. The temple that now stands was built by his devotees around it.

---

## The temple today

More than 3,000 devotees across Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, and Puducherry continue his lineage. The temple is open daily , mornings from 6:30 AM to 11:30 AM, evenings from 4:30 PM to 9:00 PM. Full moon days, Thursdays, and the monthly Magha Nakshatra are especially significant here, as Magha was the birth star of Swamigal.

Those who sit before the Samadhi are said to receive the same Jnana that Swamigal gave in life. The Diksha continues. The teaching is the same , simple, and through it, one can see the Self and be guided toward Shiva.
